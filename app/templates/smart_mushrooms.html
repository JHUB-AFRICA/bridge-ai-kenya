⚠️ The Challenge

The Problem

Mushroom farming in Kenya is commercially attractive but highly sensitive to environmental control. Small-scale and peri-urban farms often rely on manual monitoring of temperature, humidity, carbon dioxide and light. This makes it difficult to detect microclimate drift, contamination risk, poor ventilation or conditions that reduce yield.

🔥 Critical Challenges

Temperature, humidity and CO₂ drift; limited real-time data; early contamination detection; low adoption of precision tools; youth and women digital-skills gap; post-harvest losses.

🌡️ Temperature & Humidity Drift
🦠 Late Contamination Detection
📱 Low Technology Adoption
📉 Post-Harvest Losses
🍄 30-50% Yield Loss Due to suboptimal environmental conditions and manual monitoring
